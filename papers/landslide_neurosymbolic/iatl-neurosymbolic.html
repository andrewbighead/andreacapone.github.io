A Neurosymbolic Approach for Landslide Risk Assessment

Andrea Capone, Aniello Murano

ITAL-IA 2026 • Roma, Italia

Download PDF

Abstract

Landslide risk assessment remains a critical challenge, where current predictive models are still far from providing sufficiently reliable support for high-impact decisions affecting people, infrastructure, and territory. In this work, we propose a neurosymbolic architecture for landslide risk assessment that combines two neural pipelines: one for high-resolution spatial \emph{susceptibility mapping} and one for ground deformation forecasting from InSAR time series, with a symbolic layer of strategic reasoning based on Intuitionistic Alternating-time Temporal Logic (IATL). IATL is well suited to this scenario because it models the progressive refinement of information and distinguishes between what is not yet proved and what is known to be false. Through an illustrative example, we show how the symbolic layer can extend the neural component in two ways: by supporting decisions in poorly represented scenarios and by filtering physically implausible outputs. The result is a decision-support framework in which predictions are complemented by formally checkable strategic properties and inspectable reasoning traces.
